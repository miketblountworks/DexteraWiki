This Privacy Policy describes how **Dextera Labs** (“**Dextera Labs**,” “**we**,” or “**us**”) handles information when you install and use Dextera Launcher (“**Dextera**,” or “**the App**”) on your Android device.

Dextera does **not** request the Android SMS or Call Log permission groups (`READ_SMS`, call log, etc.).

Dextera is designed as a **device-local home screen launcher**. Most features run on your phone. We do **not** operate a Dextera user account system, and we do **not** sell personal information.

## 3. Information the App may access or process

Dextera only uses permissions and data needed for features you enable. Access is typically granted by you in Android Settings or during onboarding.

### 3.1 On-device information (processed locally)

Depending on permissions and settings, Dextera may process:

- **Installed applications** — labels, package names, icons, and launch intents for the app list, search, recents, and widgets  
- **Contacts** — names, phone numbers, and photos (if available) for the Contacts page, search, call/message actions, and pinning  
- **Notifications** — content delivered through Android’s notification listener (titles, text, app identity, actions) for the notification center, badges, media, and optional OTP detection  
- **Media sessions** — now-playing metadata and playback controls when media is active  
- **Usage statistics** — app usage frequency for “Frequently used” categorization (if Usage Access is granted)  
- **Local files** — names and basic file info for search when you **add folders** in Settings (only those folders). Wallpaper photos you choose are saved inside Dextera (up to seven). Dextera does not need all-photos / all-videos access.  
- **Location** (approximate or fine, if granted) — to fetch weather and related astronomical day info  
- **Network traffic counters** — aggregate upload/download rates for optional fullscreen network-speed display (not a content log of your traffic)  
- **Launcher preferences** — wallpaper, theme, clock style, fonts, page order, gesture options, search settings  
- **Security lists** — locked apps, hidden apps, pinned apps/contacts (stored with enhanced protection where implemented)  
- **Search history** (optional) — recent search selections you make when “Previous search results” is enabled  
- **Biometrics / device credential** — used via the Android system authenticator to unlock locked apps; Dextera does not receive your fingerprint/face templates  

### 3.2 Information sent over the network (only when a feature needs it)

Dextera may contact third-party services when you use related features:

| Feature | Example destinations | Data typically sent |
|---------|----------------------|---------------------|
| **Bing Daily wallpaper** | Bing image archive | Device request metadata (standard HTTP); receives wallpaper URL/image |
| **Weather** | Providers such as Open-Meteo, weather.gov (US), USNO | Coordinates or location-derived requests |
| **Web search suggestions** | Suggestion services (e.g. Google, DuckDuckGo) | Search query text you type |
| **Web search open** | Your selected engine (Google, Bing, DuckDuckGo, Brave, etc.) | Query when you open a web result |
| **General connectivity** | As needed for the above | Standard network/device headers |

Those third parties have their own privacy policies. Dextera does not control how they retain or use request data.

### 3.3 Information we do **not** intentionally collect as a cloud product

As of this policy, Dextera is not designed to:

- Create Dextera user profiles on our servers  
- Sync your contacts, SMS, notification contents, or locked-app lists to a Dextera backend  
- Sell your personal information  
- Show third-party ads based on a Dextera advertising profile  

If a future version introduces cloud accounts or telemetry, this policy will be updated before or when those features ship.

## 4. How information is used

We use the information above solely to:

- Provide home-screen, search, notifications, media, weather, and page features  
- Personalize appearance (theme colors from wallpaper, layout preferences)  
- Enforce app lock (when launching from Dextera) and uninstall-protection features you enable  
- Improve reliability of optional suggestions (web suggest, Bing wallpaper cache)  
- Remember settings you choose on the device

- **Contract / service delivery** — providing the launcher features you install and use

## 6. Storage, security, and retention

- **Storage:** Preferences and indexes are stored on your device. Sensitive launcher lists (e.g. locked/hidden/pinned packages) may use encrypted storage on-device.  
- **Retention:** Data remains until you clear app data, uninstall Dextera, or revoke permissions / delete related content on the device.  
- **Security:** Device admin and biometric app lock (when launching from Dextera) are optional protections. No method is 100% secure; physical access and OS vulnerabilities can still pose risks.  
- **Backups:** Android backup systems (if enabled on your device) may include app data according to your OS backup settings.

---

## 7. Sharing of information

Dextera shares data only as needed to operate features you use:

1. **On your device** — with Android system services (package manager, notification service, biometrics, media sessions).  
2. **Third-party network services** — wallpaper, weather, and web suggestion/search providers as described above.  
3. **When you act** — e.g. opening another app, sharing, uninstalling, or dialing a contact uses system intents to those apps/services.  

We do not sell personal information. We do not share your contacts, SMS, or notification contents with advertisers.

## 8. Children’s privacy

Dextera is a general-purpose launcher and is not directed at children under 13 (or the minimum age in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children through a Dextera cloud service. If you believe a child has provided sensitive data through optional features, clear app data or uninstall the App and contact us.

## 9. Your choices and rights

You can:

- Deny or revoke permissions in **Android Settings → Apps → Dextera → Permissions**  
- Disable notification access, usage access, or device admin in system settings  
- Turn off optional features (verification codes / OTP, search history, Bing wallpaper, network speed, etc.) in **Dextera Settings**  
- Clear app storage or uninstall Dextera to remove on-device app data  
- Choose a different web search engine or avoid web suggestions by not using search  

Depending on your location, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or restrict processing of personal data. Because most processing is on-device, exercising these rights often means managing permissions and app data on the phone. For requests related to any data that may have reached third-party services (e.g. weather or suggest providers), you may also need to contact those providers.

## 10. International processing

Network features may contact servers in other countries (for example, weather or search suggestion providers). By using those features, you understand that request data may be processed outside your country, subject to those providers’ policies and applicable law.

## 11. Device admin and sensitive permissions

Some powerful permissions require special explanation:

| Access | Why Dextera may ask |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Notification listener** | Custom notification center, badges, media awareness, OTP helpers |
| **Device admin** | Uninstallation protection (must be deactivated before uninstall) |
| **Usage access** | Frequently used apps |
| **Display over other apps** | Optional floating OTP popup |

Dextera does **not** use Accessibility services.

OTP / verification codes use the **notification listener** only (text already shown in messaging-app notifications). Dextera does not read the SMS inbox.

These are **optional** unless a feature requires them. Disabling them limits related features only.
